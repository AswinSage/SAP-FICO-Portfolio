# Introduction to Enterprise Structure

## What is Enterprise Structure?

Enterprise Structure in SAP S/4HANA is the organizational framework that represents the legal, financial, and operational structure of an organization within the SAP system.

It defines how different organizational units are arranged and connected, enabling business transactions, financial reporting, and integration across SAP modules.


## Purpose

Enterprise Structure is used to:

- Represent an organization's legal structure.
- Support financial accounting and reporting.
- Enable business process integration.
- Define responsibilities and organizational hierarchy.
- Facilitate consolidated reporting.


## Why is Enterprise Structure Important?

A properly designed Enterprise Structure helps organizations:

- Produce accurate financial statements.
- Comply with legal and statutory requirements.
- Manage multiple companies and business units.
- Integrate Finance (FI) with other SAP modules.
- Support efficient business operations.


## Key Characteristics

- Represents real-world organizational units.
- Supports multinational and multi-company organizations.
- Enables centralized and decentralized business operations.
- Forms the foundation for SAP configuration.


## Summary

Enterprise Structure is one of the first components configured in SAP because every financial and logistical transaction depends on the organizational framework defined within it.
